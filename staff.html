Staff Overview

Sammy Anim Addo

Sammy Anim Addo is a highly respected figure in Ghana's sports industry, serving as the CEO of OneWorld Sports Management, a premier agency specializing in managing football talents and offering career guidance to athletes. His expertise and deep passion for the sport has made him a key influencer in Ghanaian football, both on a national and international level.

Beyond his work with OneWorld Sports Management, Sammy Anim Addo is the bankroller and president of Young Apostles FC, a club renowned for nurturing and developing young football talent in Ghana. His leadership has been instrumental in transforming the club into a platform where aspiring footballers can thrive. Under his guidance, Young Apostles FC has gained international recognition and respect, now competing in the Ghana Premier League. Ernest Ofori, a member of Ghana's U-17 national team, is one of many notable products of the club.

Sammy is also the president of the Manchester United Ghana Supporters Union, where he fosters a strong community of Manchester United fans in Ghana, connecting grassroots football and global fandom. In addition to this, he is the president of Great Somas Academy, a model youth football club known for producing exceptional talents like Foli Adade, Mathew Cudjoe Anim, Selorm Geraldo, Mutakilu Fuseini, Samuel Gidi, and Kelvin Sabah. The academy, with numerous league honors, is recognized for having competed with and beaten the renowned Right to Dream Academy on several occasions.

As a father figure to many young players, Sammy has mentored the likes of Emmanuel Yeboah, Raymond Anokye Asante, Emmanuel Mensah, and Solomon Adomako. He has also played a pivotal role in the lives of non-football athletes, such as boxer John Laryea, whom he helped transform from a life on the streets to becoming a two-time WBO African Featherweight Champion. Sammy also spotted Ghanaian sprinter Janet Amponsah, ensuring she continued her education and went on to become a national champion in the 100m, 200m, and 4x100m events. Janet later won Commonwealth gold for Ghana and represented the country in numerous international athletics competitions.

Sammy Anim Addo's commitment to developing young talent and transforming lives through sports is evident in his extensive contributions to football, boxing, and athletics in Ghana.

Christian Weah Mombo

Christian Weah Mombo is a dynamic professional serving as the PR (Public Relations) officer for OneWorld Sports Management. In this role, he is responsible for managing the company's public image, communications, and media engagements, ensuring that the agency maintains a strong presence in the sports industry. His work plays a key role in promoting the agency's brand and its clients.

In addition to his PR duties, Christian Weah Mombo is also a certified football agent for OneWorld Sports Management, where he helps manage and negotiate deals for players, securing opportunities that align with their career goals. Furthermore, he handles the company's social media platforms, ensuring engaging content and consistent communication with fans, partners, and the global football community. His multifaceted role is essential in building the agency's success both on and off the pitch.

Jeff Ofori

Jeff Ofori, a former professional football player from Ghana, is now based in Stockholm, Sweden. He has built a successful post-playing career as a youth developer with IF Brommapojkarna, one of Sweden's most prominent football clubs known for nurturing young talent. In his current role, Jeff is deeply involved in both player scouting and coaching, working closely with young athletes to help them develop their skills and advance in their football careers.

Beyond his work at IF Brommapojkarna, Jeff has taken on an additional role as the players' trainer and adviser for OneWorld Sports Management. In this capacity, he uses his expertise to guide professional and aspiring footballers, offering advice on career development, performance enhancement, and overall well-being, ensuring that they are well-equipped for both the physical and mental demands of professional football.

Michael Kwabena Amoah

Michael Kwabena Amoah is a licensed FIFA Football Agent, registered with both the English FA and the US Soccer Federation. With a background in law and extensive experience in the IT industry, he leverages his expertise to provide comprehensive career management and strategic guidance to footballers.

As a certified Project Management Professional (PMP), Michael combines data-driven insights with a deep understanding of the football world to help players reach their full potential. His unique skill set and dedication make him a trusted advisor, ensuring success both on and off the pitch.

Matinou Inoussa

Matinou Inoussa, an ex-football player from Benin and Sweden, is currently based in Stockholm, Sweden. With 15 years of experience in football, Matinou has established himself as a youth coach and academy development manager, having worked with prominent Swedish clubs such as IF Brommapojkarna and Hammarby IF. His expertise in developing young talents and managing football academies has made him a highly regarded figure in the Swedish football community.

Matinou is now working as a football consultant, focusing on the Northern European market, particularly in Scandinavia. His specialties include player scouting and performance coaching, where he leverages his extensive experience to identify promising talent and enhance player performance. His work as a consultant allows him to contribute to the growth and success of football clubs and players across the region.
